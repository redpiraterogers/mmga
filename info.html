What Is MMGA?

MMGA (Make Meditation Great Again) is a meditation verification protocol built on Solana blockchain. Using computer vision AI, we detect two simple rules: spine erect and eyes closed. Hold both for verified time, earn $MONK tokens as proof of practice.

Every meditation app lies. They tell you to "stop your thoughts" or "clear your mind." You fail, quit, and feel broken. The truth? Your mind stills itself. You just create conditions.

The Royal Secret

The mind cannot be forced to be still.

It stills itself when you stop interfering. The body knows: erect spine, closed eyes. That's it. No mantras. No apps guiding you. Just conditions.

The Two Rules

01
SPINE ERECT

Nose above shoulders. Not rigid, not slouched. Your skeleton knows the position. When the spine is aligned, energy flows. The body relaxes into stillness.

02
EYES CLOSED

Close your eyes. Not squeezed shut, not peeking. Closed. This cuts 80% of sensory input. The mind has less to process. Stillness emerges naturally.

How It Works

  1. Open Camera: Grant camera access (processed locally, never uploaded)

  3. Verification: Hold spine erect + eyes closed for 1 second
  4. Earn MONK: Receive 1 $MONK token per verified second
  5. Mint On-Chain: Connect wallet and mint tokens to Solana blockchain

FAQ

Can I sell my $MONK tokens?

No. Tokens are non-transferable (soulbound). They're credentials showing you meditated, not currency to trade. This is intentional.

Why blockchain?

Immutable proof. No central authority can delete your practice history. Your credentials live forever, independent of any company or platform.

Is my video recorded?

No. All AI processing happens locally in your browser. No video leaves your device. We never see your camera feed.

What if I already meditate?

Perfect. This verifies what you're already doing. Earn credentials for your existing practice. No need to change your routine.

Does this track my thoughts?

No. We only verify physical conditions (posture + eye closure). Your subjective experience is yours alone.

What about lying down?

Lying down doesn't count. The protocol requires spine erect (nose above shoulders). This is intentional — the posture matters.
