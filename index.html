🔒 BIOMETRIC PRIVACY & GDPR COMPLIANCE

Client-Side Processing Architecture

MMGA uses MediaPipe Face Mesh (v0.4) and MediaPipe Pose (v0.5) — Google's open-source machine learning libraries that run entirely in the browser via WebAssembly and TensorFlow.js. All inference happens in your device's volatile RAM. No video frames, facial landmarks, pose coordinates, or biometric feature vectors are ever transmitted to any server.

Technical flow: Camera → Browser RAM (30fps inference) → Numerical validation (eyes closed Y/N, spine erect Y/N) → Timer logic → Memory wiped. The only data that leaves your device is a single integer per minute: +1 Monk Point. No pixel data. No biometric templates. Ever.

GDPR Article 9 Compliance (Biometric Data)

Under GDPR Article 9, biometric data used for "unique identification" requires explicit consent and heightened protection. MMGA does not process biometric data for identification purposes. The system detects only two binary states: eyes closed and spine position. No facial recognition. No identity matching. No biometric templates are created, stored, or transmitted.

Your GDPR rights: Right to access (nothing stored = nothing to access), Right to erasure (automatic—data never persists beyond RAM), Right to data portability (your Monk Point count is exportable), Right to object (close the modal = processing stops instantly).

Open-Source Verification

The complete MediaPipe integration code is available for public audit at github.com/mmga-protocol/biometric-detection. Independent security researchers and privacy advocates can verify that:

  • ✅ No video recording functions exist in the codebase
  • ✅ No network transmission of camera data (zero fetch() or XMLHttpRequest calls with video)
  • ✅ No localStorage or IndexedDB storage of biometric landmarks
  • ✅ Camera stream objects are explicitly destroyed on modal close

This public repository serves as our "Technical Audit" for privacy regulators. No third-party audit firm required—the code is the proof.

International Privacy Standards

GDPR (EU): No biometric processing for identification. Data minimization principle satisfied (only binary outputs, no feature vectors).
CCPA/CPRA (California): No sale of personal information. No sharing of biometric data. Opt-out is instant (close modal).

Gas Fees & Token Economics

Why do I pay fees to mint free tokens?

You're not paying MMGA—you're paying Solana network fees (similar to Ethereum gas). This is standard for all blockchain apps (OpenSea, Uniswap, Magic Eden). The token itself is free; you earn it through verified practice. The network fee (~€0.002 per mint) ensures the blockchain records your ownership permanently on-chain.

How much will 1000 mints cost me?

First mint: ~€0.33 (creates your token account)
Next 999 mints: ~€2.00 (€0.002 each)
Total: ~€2.33

That's 1000 verified meditation sessions for less than a coffee. Compare this to buying tokens (which isn't even possible during the Stillness Epoch).

Can I mint without paying anything?

No. All blockchain transactions require network fees—this is how blockchains work. However, the cost is negligible compared to the value of verified meditation practice and permanent on-chain token ownership. If network fees are prohibitive, consider batch minting (mint multiple tokens at once to amortize costs).

Where do the fees go?

100% to Solana validators (the computers that run the blockchain). MMGA receives €0 from minting transactions. This is not a revenue stream—it's network infrastructure. Think of it like postal stamps: you pay the postal service to deliver a letter, not the person who wrote it.

What about the Pro subscription (€9/month)?

Pro subscription is for content access only: guided sessions, advanced analytics, tutorials, community features. It does NOT increase your minting rate, give you extra tokens, or bypass gas fees. You can mint unlimited tokens on the free tier—you just won't have access to premium educational content.

Is this legally compliant?

Yes. Network fees paid to blockchain validators are not considered capital contributions under securities law (SEC guidance). You're paying for infrastructure (like electricity for Bitcoin mining), not investing in MMGA. The token is earned through practice, not purchased. MMGA receives zero revenue from minting transactions.
